12-shot protocol — BTE hearing aid

6 orientations × 2 aids (L then R) = 12 shots per pair. Device on the table, credit card in frame as scale reference. Camera always straight down.

Shoot order: Always L aid first (all 6 orientations 1→6), then R aid (all 6 orientations 7→12). Same anatomical axis sequence both times.
Anatomical naming: medial = inner (touches head, label-side); lateral = outer; anterior = front edge; posterior = back edge; superior = top end (hook); inferior = bottom end (shell base).

1. Lateral face up

L: shot 1·R: shot 7
Outer shell up. No label visible.
Camera view

2. Medial face up

L: shot 2·R: shot 8
Inner side up. Label visible.
Camera view

3. Anterior edge up

L: shot 3·R: shot 9
Device on its posterior edge. Front edge faces camera.
Camera view
Thin edge-on silhouette — narrow profile, hook visible curving away.

4. Posterior edge up

L: shot 4·R: shot 10
Device on its anterior edge. Back edge faces camera.
Camera view
Same thin silhouette, hook hidden behind (no protrusion).

5. Superior end up

L: shot 5·R: shot 11
Device standing on inferior end. Hook attachment faces camera.
Camera view
Oval cross-section (wider than tall) with hook stub.

6. Inferior end up

L: shot 6·R: shot 12
Device standing on superior end. Bottom of shell faces camera.
Camera view
Full cross-section showing the battery door.

Card visible in every shot. Some orientations are physically awkward — BTEs don't balance well on edges or ends — just hold them with a finger and shoot whatever silhouette you can.
