Android Distribution Runbook

How a Recycled Sound build gets from this repo onto a volunteer's Android phone, the iOS-TestFlight counterpart. Mirrors co.enspyr.recycledsound to a Play internal testing track via the Android Publisher API.

What only you can do (Console, your Google login)

Three things gate the first rollout. None are automatable — they need your account auth and policy answers.

1. Grant the publisher service account (1 minute)

Play Console → Users & permissions → Invite new users → paste:

play-publisher@recycled-sound-app.iam.gserviceaccount.com

Grant app access to Recycled Sound with at least Release to testing tracks (or Admin for simplicity). This is what flips the 403 to a working upload.

2. Complete "Set up your app" content (one-time, ~20 min)

Play blocks every rollout — even internal — until these are answered:

  • Privacy policy URL — we already have one (docs/privacy.html); paste its hosted URL.
  • Data safety form — what the app collects (camera images, anonymous auth id, device specs).
  • Content rating questionnaire.
  • Target audience + Ads declaration (no ads).
3. Add internal testers + share the opt-in link

Play Console → Testing → Internal testing → Testers → add a list of emails (Delia + the dress-rehearsal crew). Play generates a shareable opt-in link — no per-tester developer invite, the TestFlight-equivalent "join" URL.

What's now one command (after the grant)

Build a fresh signed AAB and push it to the internal track:

cd recycled_sound && flutter build appbundle --release
cd .. && python3 scripts/play_upload.py \
  --aab recycled_sound/build/app/outputs/bundle/release/app-release.aab \
  --track internal --status completed

Use --status draft for a dry run that stages without releasing. Auth resolves from ~/.claude/keys/recycled-sound-play-publisher.json (override with $RS_PLAY_SA_KEY).

Notes & gotchas
  • versionCode must increase every upload. Driven by pubspec version: x.y.z+N (currently 0.5.0+8). Bump +N before each new upload.
  • AAB is ~97 MB (bundled TFLite + ML Kit models). Play splits it per-device, so the actual download is much smaller.
  • Google Sign-In would need the upload-key SHA-1 (7D:40:93:A9:…) registered in Firebase — not needed today (anonymous auth).
  • Keystore is resettable under Play App Signing; losing the upload key is recoverable, unlike legacy signing.
