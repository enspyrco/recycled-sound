## SECTION 2: Project Information

### Project Title (max 10 words)

**Recycled Sound: Hearing Aid Reuse for Greater Dandenong**

> **Word count: 8**

### Project Summary (10–150 words)

Recycled Sound is a new initiative by the Rotary Club of Springvale City, in
partnership with Arches Audiology, to collect, service, and redistribute donated hearing
aids to Greater Dandenong residents who face barriers to hearing support. The project
targets people on low incomes, asylum seekers, and refugees who cannot access or afford
hearing devices through existing pathways.

Operating primarily from the Arches Audiology clinic in Mulgrave, the project will
establish a sustainable hearing aid reuse program including: professional servicing and
calibration of donated devices, community awareness and collection campaigns, and a
clinical pathway to match reconditioned hearing aids with residents in need. We expect
to support approximately 80 people over the 12-month project period, with the help of
audiology student volunteers and Rotary members.

## SECTION 5: Project Rationale

### Rationale: What is the need or desire in the community that the project will address? (10–250 words)

Hearing loss is one of the most prevalent chronic health conditions in Australia,
affecting an estimated 3.6 million people nationally. In Greater Dandenong — one of
Victoria's most culturally diverse and socioeconomically disadvantaged municipalities —
barriers to hearing care are compounded by cost, language, and limited awareness of
available services.

Many residents, particularly refugees, asylum seekers, and people on low incomes, are
ineligible for government-subsidised hearing aids through Hearing Australia, or face
long wait times. Privately purchased hearing aids can cost between $2,000 and $8,000
per pair, putting them out of reach for many.

At the same time, thousands of functioning hearing aids are discarded each year when
owners pass away, upgrade, or no longer need them. These devices still have years of
useful life but currently have no structured pathway for reuse in the local community.

Recycled Sound addresses both problems: it captures donated hearing aids that would
otherwise go to waste, and channels them — after professional servicing and calibration
— to Greater Dandenong residents who need them most. The project responds directly to
the community's need for affordable, accessible hearing support while promoting a
sustainable reuse model.

> **Word count: 178**

### Evidence: How did you identify the need for this project? (10–250 words)

The need for this project was identified through several converging sources:

**Direct community observation:** Members of the Rotary Club of Springvale City,
through years of local service, have observed that hearing loss is a significant but
often overlooked barrier to social participation, particularly among older residents
and newly arrived refugee communities.

**Clinical experience:** Seray Lim, founder of Arches Audiology and a Rotary Club of
Springvale City member, has over a decade of experience in community health and
not-for-profit audiology. Through her clinical practice, she regularly encounters
patients who cannot afford hearing aids and have no viable pathway to access them.

**Existing donated devices:** The Rotary Club has already begun collecting donated
hearing aids, with approximately 20 devices currently on a register. Brands include
Unitron, Phonak, Oticon, Signia, GN Resound, Beltone, and Widex. Most of these
devices require professional servicing and calibration before they can be safely
redistributed — work that requires specialist equipment and expertise that the grant
will fund.

**National data:** The Hearing Care Industry Association estimates that Australians
discard thousands of hearing aids annually. There is currently no widely accessible
community-level reuse program in the Greater Dandenong area.

This combination of community need, clinical expertise, existing donated stock, and a
willing partnership between Rotary and Arches Audiology makes this project both timely
and feasible.

## SECTION 7: Capacity and Risk (10–150 words)

### How will you ensure that your project is delivered on time and within budget? Please include any relevant organisational experience or expertise and how you plan to manage project risks.

The Rotary Club of Springvale City has a strong track record of delivering community
service projects, supported by an experienced committee structure including dedicated
Community, Finance, and Project leads. Our partnership with Arches Audiology provides
specialist clinical expertise — founder Seray Lim is a qualified Clinical Audiologist
with experience in community health and not-for-profit audiology, and an Independent
Audiologists Australia Community Champion Award recipient.

Key risk mitigation strategies include: a dedicated project register to track all
donated devices from intake through to distribution; quarterly budget reviews by the
club Treasurer; clinical oversight by a qualified audiologist for all device servicing;
and a volunteer coordination plan to ensure consistent audiology student support
throughout the project period. If device supply exceeds servicing capacity, we will
prioritise based on recipient need.
