## 3. PROJECT DETAILS — RECYCLED SOUND

### Project Name
**Recycled Sound**

### Project Tagline Options
- "Hearing Aid Reuse for Greater Dandenong"
- "Giving the gift of hearing through reuse"
- "Reducing barriers to hearing support"

### Project Purpose (one sentence)
Recycled Sound collects, services, and redistributes donated hearing aids to Greater
Dandenong residents who face barriers to hearing support, particularly people on low
incomes, asylum seekers, and refugees.

### Four Pillars
1. **Reducing barriers** to hearing support
2. **Increasing access** to reused/recycled hearing devices
3. **Strengthening social connection** for residents
4. **Sustainability** — reuse model rather than disposal

### Target Beneficiaries
- People with hearing loss
- People on low incomes
- Asylum seekers and refugees
- Older adults / seniors
- CALD (Culturally and Linguistically Diverse) communities

### Target Area
- City of Greater Dandenong, Victoria
- Primary clinic: Mulgrave (within Greater Dandenong)
- Potential outreach: Springvale, Dandenong, Noble Park

### Project Activities
1. Hearing aid collection and donation drives
2. Professional assessment, servicing, and calibration of donated devices
3. Community awareness and marketing campaigns (multilingual)
4. Clinical fitting and distribution to eligible recipients
5. Data collection, monitoring, and evaluation
6. Recruitment and coordination of audiology student volunteers

### Volunteer Model
- Audiology students from University of Melbourne and La Trobe University
- Allied health assistance volunteers
- Approximately 1 day per week commitment
- Rotary member volunteers for coordination and outreach

### Key Statistics for Applications
- **Expected beneficiaries:** 80 people per year
- **Volunteers:** ~15 (Rotary members + students)
- **Volunteer hours:** ~400 per year
- **Devices already collected:** ~20 (as of March 2026)

## 5. EVIDENCE BASE — REUSABLE STATISTICS AND ARGUMENTS

### Hearing Loss in Australia
- Approximately 3.6 million Australians are affected by hearing loss
- Hearing loss is one of the most prevalent chronic health conditions nationally
- Untreated hearing loss is linked to social isolation, depression, cognitive decline,
  and reduced quality of life
- Thousands of functioning hearing aids are discarded annually when owners pass away,
  upgrade, or no longer need them

### Cost Barriers
- Privately purchased hearing aids cost between $2,000 and $8,000 per pair
- Many residents are ineligible for government-subsidised devices through Hearing
  Australia, or face long wait times
- Asylum seekers and some visa holders are excluded from subsidised hearing programs

### Greater Dandenong Context
- One of Victoria's most culturally diverse municipalities
- One of Victoria's most socioeconomically disadvantaged municipalities
- High refugee and asylum seeker population
- Barriers to hearing care compounded by cost, language, and limited awareness
- No widely accessible community-level hearing aid reuse program exists in the area

### Why Reuse Works
- Donated hearing aids still have years of useful life
- Professional servicing and calibration can restore them to safe, effective condition
- Reuse is environmentally sustainable (reduces e-waste)
- Dramatically reduces cost to recipient (free or minimal charge)
- Creates a circular economy model for medical devices
